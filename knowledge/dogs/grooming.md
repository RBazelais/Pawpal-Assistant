# Dog Grooming

- Brushing frequency depends on coat type: daily for long/double coats, weekly for short coats, every few days for medium coats.
- Regular brushing distributes natural oils, reduces shedding, and prevents painful matting before it starts.
- Dogs should be bathed every 4–6 weeks on average; over-bathing strips natural oils and can cause dry, itchy skin.
- Nail trimming is needed every 3–4 weeks — nails that touch the ground when standing are too long and affect posture over time.
- The quick (blood vessel inside the nail) is visible as a pink area in light-colored nails; in dark nails, trim small increments to avoid cutting it.
- Ears should be checked weekly for redness, odor, or discharge; floppy-eared breeds (Basset, Cocker) need more frequent cleaning.
- Teeth should be brushed with dog-safe toothpaste at least 2–3 times per week to prevent periodontal disease.
- Eye discharge ("sleep" or "goop") is normal in small amounts; excess, colored, or crusty buildup warrants attention.
- Double-coated breeds (Husky, Golden, Shepherd) should never be shaved — the undercoat regulates temperature in both heat and cold.
- Professional grooming every 6–8 weeks is standard for breeds with continuously growing coats (Poodle, Doodle, Shih Tzu).
- Paw pads should be inspected regularly for cracks, cuts, or debris, especially after walks on hot pavement or salted roads in winter.
- Start grooming routines early in puppyhood so dogs learn to tolerate handling of paws, ears, and mouth.
