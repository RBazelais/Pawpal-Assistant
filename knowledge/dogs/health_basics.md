# Dog Health Basics — Minor Wellness & Home Care

- Pumpkin puree (plain, not pie filling) is a well-established dietary remedy for mild digestive upset in dogs — 1–4 teaspoons mixed into food can help firm up loose stools or soothe an upset stomach due to its soluble fiber content. (Source: AKC, veterinary nutrition guidelines)
- Dogs occasionally vomit after eating too fast, eating grass, or switching foods — this is usually not cause for alarm if it happens rarely and the dog remains alert and active.
- Slow-feeder bowls or spreading kibble on a snuffle mat can reduce gulping and prevent post-meal vomiting in dogs that eat too quickly.
- A single episode of soft stool can result from diet change, stress, or eating something unusual; withholding food for 12 hours then offering a bland diet (boiled chicken and plain white rice) is a commonly recommended first step.
- Persistent or bloody diarrhea, repeated vomiting, or vomiting combined with lethargy always requires a veterinarian — home care is only appropriate for mild, isolated incidents.
- Dental chews and bones approved by the Veterinary Oral Health Council (VOHC) can help reduce plaque and tartar buildup between professional cleanings.
- Dogs that eat grass occasionally are generally doing so out of curiosity or to add fiber — it is rarely a sign of illness unless accompanied by other symptoms.
- Keeping a dog's weight in a healthy range reduces risk of joint disease, diabetes, and digestive issues; a rib-check (you should feel but not see ribs) is a simple at-home body condition gauge.
- Regular exercise supports healthy digestion — a daily walk helps maintain gut motility in dogs.
- Signs of a minor upset stomach that can be monitored at home: one or two vomiting episodes, normal energy level, drinking water, no blood present.
