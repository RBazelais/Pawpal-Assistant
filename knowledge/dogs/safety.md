# Dog Safety — Toxic Foods & Plants, Safe Alternatives

## Foods toxic to dogs
- Chocolate is toxic to dogs — it contains theobromine, which dogs metabolize slowly. Dark chocolate and baking chocolate are the most dangerous; even small amounts can cause vomiting, diarrhea, tremors, or seizures. (Source: ASPCA Animal Poison Control)
- Grapes and raisins can cause acute kidney failure in dogs even in small quantities; the toxic compound is not fully identified, so no amount is considered safe. (Source: ASPCA)
- Onions, garlic, leeks, and chives (raw, cooked, or powdered) damage red blood cells and can cause anemia in dogs with repeated or large exposure. (Source: AKC, ASPCA)
- Xylitol (an artificial sweetener found in sugar-free gum, candy, peanut butter, and baked goods) causes a rapid insulin spike and can lead to liver failure in dogs. Always check peanut butter labels before giving to dogs. (Source: FDA, ASPCA)
- Macadamia nuts cause weakness, vomiting, tremors, and hyperthermia in dogs. (Source: ASPCA)
- Avocado contains persin, which can cause vomiting and diarrhea in dogs, especially the skin and pit. (Source: ASPCA)
- Alcohol and caffeine (coffee, tea, energy drinks) are toxic to dogs and should never be given intentionally or left within reach. (Source: ASPCA)
- Raw yeast dough expands in a dog's stomach and produces alcohol as it ferments, causing bloating and alcohol toxicity. (Source: AKC)

## Safe food alternatives dogs can enjoy
- Plain cooked chicken (no seasoning, no bones) is a safe, high-protein treat and a commonly recommended bland diet ingredient.
- Carrots are a low-calorie, crunchy treat that most dogs enjoy and that supports dental health; safe raw or cooked. (Source: AKC)
- Blueberries are a safe, antioxidant-rich snack for dogs in moderate amounts.
- Watermelon (seedless, no rind) is safe and hydrating for dogs as an occasional treat.
- Plain cooked sweet potato is a fiber-rich, safe treat; always cooked and without seasoning.
- Plain air-popped popcorn (no butter, no salt) is safe in small amounts as an occasional snack.
- Apples (sliced, seeds and core removed) are safe and many dogs enjoy the crunch.

## Plants toxic to dogs
- Sago palm is extremely toxic to dogs — all parts are poisonous, and ingestion can cause liver failure and death. (Source: ASPCA)
- Azalea and rhododendron ingestion can cause vomiting, drooling, heart issues, and coma in dogs. (Source: ASPCA)
- Tulip and hyacinth bulbs are toxic to dogs; the bulb is the most concentrated source and causes GI upset and central nervous system effects. (Source: ASPCA)
- Autumn crocus (Colchicum) is highly toxic and can cause multi-organ failure in dogs. (Source: ASPCA)
- Lilies of the valley cause heart arrhythmias in dogs; not as dangerous as true lilies are for cats, but still harmful. (Source: ASPCA)
