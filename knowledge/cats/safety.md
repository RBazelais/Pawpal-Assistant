# Cat Safety — Toxic Plants, Toxic Foods & Safe Alternatives

## Plants toxic to cats
- True lilies (Easter lily, Tiger lily, Asiatic lily, Day lily) are extremely toxic to cats — even small amounts of pollen, petals, or water from the vase can cause acute kidney failure. These should never be in a home with cats. (Source: ASPCA, Cornell Feline Health Center)
- Sago palm is highly toxic to cats; all parts cause liver failure and can be fatal. (Source: ASPCA)
- Azalea and rhododendron cause vomiting, drooling, low blood pressure, and heart arrhythmias in cats. (Source: ASPCA)
- Tulip and hyacinth bulbs are toxic to cats and cause gastrointestinal irritation, drooling, and central nervous system effects. (Source: ASPCA)
- Pothos, philodendron, and dieffenbachia (common houseplants) contain calcium oxalate crystals that cause oral pain, drooling, and GI irritation in cats. (Source: ASPCA)
- Autumn crocus is one of the most dangerous plants for cats, causing multi-organ failure. (Source: ASPCA)
- English ivy (and most ivy varieties) causes GI upset and neurological effects in cats. (Source: ASPCA)
- Aloe vera is mildly toxic to cats, causing vomiting and lethargy if ingested. (Source: ASPCA)

## Foods toxic to cats
- Onions, garlic, leeks, and chives (in any form) destroy red blood cells in cats and can cause hemolytic anemia; cats are more sensitive to these than dogs. (Source: ASPCA)
- Chocolate and caffeine contain theobromine and caffeine which are toxic to cats, causing tremors, seizures, and heart issues. (Source: ASPCA)
- Xylitol (artificial sweetener) is toxic to cats; while less studied than in dogs, it should be avoided entirely. (Source: ASPCA)
- Raw fish fed regularly can deplete thiamine (vitamin B1) in cats, leading to neurological problems. (Source: Cornell Feline Health Center)
- Alcohol is toxic to cats in even tiny amounts and should never be within reach. (Source: ASPCA)
- Grapes and raisins have an unclear but potentially harmful effect in cats; best avoided entirely. (Source: ASPCA)

## Safe food treats cats can enjoy
- Plain cooked chicken or turkey (no seasoning, no bones) is a safe, protein-rich treat most cats love.
- Plain cooked salmon or tuna is safe as an occasional treat; not as a dietary staple due to mercury content.
- Small amounts of plain cooked egg are safe for cats and provide protein.
- Cooked plain pumpkin (not spiced) is safe and can help with minor digestive issues.
- Most cats are lactose intolerant as adults, so milk and dairy should be given in very small amounts or avoided.

## Cat-safe houseplants (non-toxic alternatives)
- Spider plant, Boston fern, Calathea, Areca palm, and African violet are all non-toxic to cats and commonly recommended as safe houseplants. (Source: ASPCA non-toxic plant list)
